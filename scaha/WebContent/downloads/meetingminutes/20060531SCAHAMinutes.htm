GUEST

Robert Doherty introduced James Cefaly of LA Kings Fan Development Department who is the liaison for local hockey in Southern California. Bob prefaced James’ presentation by giving a small synopsis of the different programs that the Kings and Ducks have begun in collaboration with member SCAHA clubs.

 

James Cefaly recapped the informal meetings that were held with SCAHA and CAHA. The basic question asked was “What can we do and what can be done to grow hockey in Southern California?” Some of the ideas that have been put in place are: CAHA/SCAHA announcements on the Kings’ jumbotron during games, booths set up at games where information for SCAHA and CAHA is handed out and the distribution of street hockey sets to elementary schools. James went on to say that with the help of LeAnn Doherty they were able to implement the street hockey program at Foothill Ranch elementary school. Over 700 children as well as teachers were taught the basics as part of the PE/after school program. It was a two-day affair and it was very successful. The Kings are in the process of building this program by getting more equipment to distribute. Their only limitation is personnel to go to the schools to demonstrate and teach the basics. James added that ideally they would like to expand the program to include elementary schools in each geographic quadrant.

 

Other programs include “Try hockey for free” at Easy Street. The program is six weeks long for children who have never played before. Equipment is donated, sticks from Easton and jerseys from the Kings. It’s a simple program but very effective. Currently Easy Street has 54 children enrolled.

 

James thanked the SCAHA board.
